Some of My Favorite Fishing Spots in Colorado

Colorado is one of the best states in the country for trout fishing, with hundreds of lakes, reservoirs, and gold-medal rivers to choose from. Here are a few of my personal favorites.

Lakes

Indian Peaks Wilderness

The Indian Peaks Wilderness west of Boulder is home to dozens of alpine lakes tucked into the mountains. These high-country gems are loaded with cutthroat, brook trout, and rainbows. The hike in can be long but it is worth every step!

Rivers & Streams

South Platte River

One of Colorado's premier Gold Medal rivers, the South Platte runs through Cheesman Canyon and Eleven Mile Canyon with some of the biggest brown trout, brook trout, and rainbows in the state. Technical nymphing and dry-fly fishing at its finest.

South Saint Vrain Creek

A beautiful freestone stream in the foothills above Lyons, the South Saint Vrain offers classic wild trout fishing in a stunning canyon setting. Great for wade fishing with light tackle or a 4-weight.

Blue River

Running through the heart of Summit County below Dillon Reservoir, the Blue River is stocked with rainbows and holds wild browns in its deeper pools. Easy access and consistent action make it a local favorite.

What to Bring

  • Colorado fishing license (They'll get ya eventually!)
  • 4–6 weight fly rod or light spinning gear
  • Flies and/or lures
  • Polarized sunglasses to spot fish
  • Waders and wading boots for rivers
  • Plenty of water and sunscreen. The altitude is no joke..
